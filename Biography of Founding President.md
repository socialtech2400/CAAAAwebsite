华强律师介绍

华强律师执业法律二十三年，始终以专业力量维护在美华裔权益，推动多元共融。

华强律师持有美国加利福尼亚州律师执业资格，并具备美国联邦多个法院的出庭资格，包括加州中区地方法院、第九巡回上诉法院、联邦巡回上诉法院、联邦最高法院及美国国际贸易法院。

华强律师目前担任美国律师协会国际法部移民法分会副主席、美国移民律师协会网络评审委员会委员，并曾于2013至2015年期间出任加州律师协会驻美国律师协会议会代表。此外，华强律师还担任美国华人全国委员会法律委员会执行副主席及加州亚裔律师协会会长。

在专业领域，华强律师已处理近千件移民及民事案件，业务范围涵盖跨国遗产继承、集体诉讼、各类非移民与移民签证申请，学生违纪违规的案件和刑事辩护案件。华强律师屡次获邀给美国律师协会、加州律师公会、洛杉矶律师公会，以及上海律师协会、江苏律师协会， 加州地产协会，美国商务部等权威专业组织发表演讲，其移民法相关学术论文亦发表于国际性律师期刊。

2016年8月，华强律师在美国律师协会议会在旧金山举行的会议上发表公开演讲，呼吁代表支持反歧视与反霸凌议案。该提案最终获500余名代表投票通过，成为美国律师协会纪律规范第8.4条的示范条例。华强律师曾获美国律师协会嘉奖。

在社区贡献方面，自2016年2月起，华强律师持续致力于维护在美华裔群体的合法权益。她曾参与“挺梁”运动，并就多项社会议题积极发声，包括致函谷歌首席执行官要求下架涉嫌煽动针对华人犯罪的歌曲《Meet the Flockers》；向加州州长建言反对对亚裔进行细分分类；去信切萨皮克警察局长督促追查陈建生案凶手；揭露费城中餐馆业主所受不公待遇；以及致函美联航首席执行官与美国交通部长赵小兰，就亚裔乘客陶大伟被拖拽事件提出严正抗议，并代表200余名乘客发起集体维权行动。2018年2月，华强律师联名致信美国国会参众两院领袖，呼吁公平对待华裔社群、反对种族歧视。同年6月，华强律师入选美国律师协会少数族裔律师领袖培训项目。

华强律师于2022年10月22日荣获美国华人华侨联合总会Acua 颁发的的木兰奖。 她在获奖致辞中呼吁华裔群体超越党派界限、团结协作，共同推动建设公平和谐的社会环境，确保美国宪法赋予的权利惠及每一位华裔人士。

2023年12月12日，值《排华法案》废除八十周年之际，华强律师在全美社区领袖会议上指出：“美国社会更崇尚实力。唯有华裔群体凝聚共识、明确方向，不断提升政治、社会及经济地位，方能防止历史重演，使宪法精神真正照耀我们及后代的前途。”

2025年1月，华强律师以特邀嘉宾身份出席全美亚裔律师协会会议，并在发言中指出当前美国立法、司法及行政系统中尚无大陆出生华裔担任高层职位的现状。华强律师强调华裔应积极争取突破该“零的局面”，以弥合经济地位与社会影响力之间的差距。

2025 年9 月27 日，世界阳光女人联合总会给华强律师颁发了世界杰出亚裔女性奖。在颁奖典礼上， 华强律师提到她将继续为打破偏见、提升华裔话语权而行动，让法治之光惠及每一个群体和他们的子孙后代，把为平等而抗争的精神传承下去。

华强律师对正义与公平的执着追求，贯穿于她有影响力的法律实践与社区服务之中，她的梦想就是让在美华裔有尊严地活着。她不仅改变了当事人的人生，也为她所服务的社群带来了深远的影响。

华强律师现在担任加州亚裔律师协会的会长。 加州亚裔律师协会旨在为亚裔美国法律从业者赋能，并在法律界及更广泛的社会领域中推动正义、平等与包容。提升加州亚裔律师的专业发展、领导力和行业影响力。为在美亚裔撑起法律保护伞。本协会亦致力于为亚裔弱势社群提供法律讲座和可负担的法律服务。

Attorney Qiang Bjornbak has practiced law for 23 years, consistently utilizing her professional expertise to protect the rights of Chinese Americans and promote diversity and inclusion. She is licensed to practice law in the State of California and is admitted to appear before several federal courts, including the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California, the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit, the Federal Circuit, the U.S. Supreme Court, and the U.S. Court of International Trade.

Attorney Qiang Bjornbak currently serves as the Vice Chair of the Immigration Law Committee of the American Bar Association (ABA) International Law Section and as a member of the American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA) Online Course Review Committee. From 2013 to 2015, she served as the California State Bar delegate to the ABA House of Delegates. Additionally, she serves as the Executive Vice Chair and the Legal Counsel for National Council of Chinese Americans (NCCA) and as the President of the Asian American Attorneys Association of California.

In her professional practice, Attorney Bjornbak has handled nearly a thousand immigration and civil cases. Her expertise spans cross-border inheritance, class action lawsuits, various non-immigrant and immigrant visa applications, student disciplinary cases, and criminal defense. She has been frequently invited to speak before prestigious professional organizations, including the ABA, the State Bar of California, the Los Angeles County Bar Association, the Shanghai Bar Association, the Jiangsu Bar Association, the California Association of Realtors, and the U.S. Department of Commerce. Her scholarly articles on immigration law have been published in “the International Lawyers”.

In August 2016, during the ABA House of Delegates meeting in San Francisco, Attorney Bjornbak delivered a public speech urging delegates to support an anti-discrimination and anti-bullying resolution. The proposal was ultimately passed by more than 500 delegates, becoming Model Rule 8.4 of the ABA Rules of Professional Conduct. She has previously received an award from the American Bar Association.

Regarding community contribution, Attorney Bjornbak has been dedicated to defending the legal rights of the Chinese American community since February 2016. She participated in the "Peter Liang" movement and has actively spoken out on numerous social issues, including: writing to the CEO of Google to demand the removal of "Meet the Flockers," a song suspected of inciting violence against Chinese people; advising the Governor of California against ethnic disaggregation of Asian Americans; writing to the Chesapeake Police Chief to urge the prosecution of the perpetrator in the Jiansheng Chen’s case; exposing the unfair treatment of Chinese restaurant owners in Philadelphia; and writing to the CEO of United Airlines and U.S. Secretary of Transportation Elaine Chao to formally protest the dragging of Asian passenger Dr. David Dao, while representing over 200 passengers in a collective advocacy action. In February 2018, she co-signed a letter to leaders of the U.S. Senate and House of Representatives calling for the fair treatment of the Chinese community and opposing racial discrimination. In June of the same year, she was selected for the ABA’s Collective Bargaining Leaders Academy.

On October 22, 2022, Attorney Qiang Bjornbak was honored with the Mulan Award by the American Chinese United Association (ACUA). In her acceptance speech, she called upon the Chinese community to look beyond partisan lines and work together to build a fair and harmonious social environment, ensuring that the rights granted by the U.S. Constitution benefit every person of Chinese descent.

On December 12, 2023, marking the 80th anniversary of the repeal of the Chinese Exclusion Act, Attorney Bjornbak noted at a national community leaders conference: "American society values strength. Only when the Chinese community builds consensus, defines its direction, and continuously elevates its political, social, and economic status can we prevent history from repeating itself and ensure the spirit of the Constitution truly illuminates the future for us and our descendants."

In January 2025, Attorney Bjornbak attended the American Asian Attorneys Association  (AAAA) meeting as a keynote speaker. In her remarks, she pointed out the current absence of Mainland-born Chinese Americans in high-level positions within the U.S. legislative, judicial, and executive systems. She emphasized that Chinese Americans should actively strive to break this "zero-occupancy" status to bridge the gap between their economic standing and social influence.

On September 27, 2025, the World Sunshine Women Federation presented Attorney Qiang Bjornbak with the Outstanding Asian Woman of the World Award. At the ceremony, she mentioned that she will continue to act to break prejudices and enhance the voice of the Chinese community, allowing the rule of law to benefit every group and their future generations, while passing down the spirit of fighting for equality.

Attorney Qiang Bjornbak’s persistent pursuit of justice and fairness is woven throughout her influential legal practice and community service. Her dream is for Chinese Americans to live with dignity. She has not only changed the lives of her clients but has also brought about a profound impact on the communities she serves.

Attorney Qiang Bjornbak currently serves as the President of the California Asian American Attorneys Association. (CAAAA) The association aims to empower Asian American legal practitioners and promote justice, equality, and inclusion within the legal profession and broader society. It seeks to enhance the professional development, leadership, and industry influence of Asian American lawyers in California, providing a legal umbrella of protection for the Asian American community. The association is also dedicated to providing legal seminars and affordable legal services to vulnerable members of the Asian community.
